# Ecommerce Strategic Analysis Summary (2020–2024)

## 🔹 0. Executive Overview
The analysis covers key aspects of the business: from overall revenue to drivers, risks, and business sustainability.  
Main structure:  
1. Revenue & Growth → assess financial health  
2. Product Performance → what drives revenue  
3. Customer Value → who generates revenue  
4. Geography → operational differences  
5. Payment & Order Success → revenue losses due to cancellations  
6. Discount & Margin → impact of discounts on margin  
7. Segmentation → revenue dependence on a small group  
8. Retention → loyalty and repeat purchases  

---

## 1. Revenue & Growth

### 1.1 Monthly Revenue Trend
- Stable revenue of 1.4M–1.6M per month over 5 years.
- Business is in a maturity phase: stable but not actively growing.

### 1.2 Seasonality Patterns
- Lowest revenue in February; peaks in summer and year-end months.
- Predictable cycles allow for promotion and inventory planning.

### 1.3 Gross vs Net Revenue (Discount Impact)
- Minimal difference between gross and net revenue → discounts are not a key driver.

### 1.4 Estimated Profit (Revenue – Shipping Cost)
- Shipping costs ~0.8–1% → margin is controlled.

### Strategic Interpretation
- The business is financially stable but lacks growth momentum.
- Recommendations: increase AOV, acquire new customers, strengthen retention strategy.

---

## 2. Product Performance

### 2.1 Top 10 Products by Revenue
- Revenue is evenly distributed (1.88–1.93M), no “hero product.”
- Low concentration risk but limited potential for rapid growth.

### 2.2 Revenue by Category

- **Electronics** is the dominant category (~47.8M), generating more than half of total revenue and acting as the primary growth driver.
- **Home & Kitchen** follows at a significant distance (~18.5M).
- Mid-tier contributors include **Clothing** (~9.2M) and **Sports & Outdoors** (~7.3M).
- **Toys & Games** (~5.6M) and **Books** (~3.5M) represent smaller but stable revenue streams.

Revenue is heavily concentrated in Electronics, increasing performance leverage but also category dependency risk.


### Strategic Interpretation
- Lack of a strong category/product driver partially explains stable revenue trends.

---

## 3. Customer Value

### 3.1 Top 10 Customers by Total Spend
- Top 10 customers: 540K–572K each.
- Low dependency on a single client.

### 3.2 Average Order Value (AOV)
- AOV ≈ 918 → high transaction value.
- Retention and repeat purchase behavior are critical.

### Strategic Interpretation
- High AOV → focus on retaining existing customers is crucial.

---

## 4. Geographic Analysis

### 4.1 Revenue by State
- Leaders: TX (~23M), CA (~18M)
- Other states: ~4–5M

### 4.2 Average Shipping Cost by State
- NY, NC, OH, PA, IL — highest (~7.47–7.50 USD)
- FL, DC, IN — lowest (~7.28–7.33 USD)

### 4.3 Cancellation Rate by State
- IN (3.69%), FL (3.32%), NY (3.22%) — highest
- NC (2.55%), WA (2.66%) — lowest
- Average rate ~3% — stable.

### Insights
- Focus on TX and CA for marketing and operational optimization.
- IN and FL — potential for cancellation reduction measures.

---

## 5. Payment & Order Success

### 5.1 Revenue by Payment Method
- Leader: Credit Card (~32.1M), followed by Debit Card, UPI/Amazon Pay.
- Cash on Delivery: ~4.5M, slightly higher cancellation risk.

### 5.2 Cancellation Rate
- Overall ~2.9–3.2%
- Cash on Delivery slightly higher (3.17%)

### 5.3 Monthly Cancellation Trend
- Fluctuations ~2.1–4%
- Short-term peaks require investigation.

### Key Insights
- Main revenue comes from card and online payments.
- Cash on Delivery is riskier; incentives for online payment could help.
- Stable cancellation rates allow reliable revenue forecasting.

---

## 6. Discount & Margin Impact

### 6.1 Total Discount Given
- Discounts ≈ 7,422.60 on total revenue ~91.8M → minimal impact.

### 6.2 Monthly Discount Impact
- Stable ~106–135/month.
- Peaks: January, some summer months (seasonal promotions).

### Insights
- Discounts are a controlled tool, not an aggressive revenue driver.
- Optimizing minor discounts could improve margin without reducing sales.

---

## 7. Customer Segmentation (RFM-lite)

### 7.1 Purchase Frequency & Spend
- Top customers: 5–10 purchases, 8–15K spend.
- Some customers haven’t purchased recently → potential for reactivation.

### 7.2 Revenue Contribution by Quintile
- Top 20% → 44% of revenue (~40.2M)
- Second quintile → 26%, third → 17%, fourth → 10%, fifth → 3–4%

### Key Insights
- Top customers are critical for revenue.
- Segmentation allows personalized retention campaigns.
- Monitoring last purchase dates helps prevent customer churn.

---

## 8. Retention & Repeat Purchase Rate

### 8.1 Overall Repeat Purchase Rate
- 68.7% → majority of customers make repeat purchases.

### 8.2 Monthly Repeat Purchase Rate
- 1–2%/month, peaks up to 2–2.5% (Nov 2020, Jul 2021)
- Minimum: Aug 2024 (1.05%), Feb 2024 (1.10%), Dec 2023 (1.23%)

### Key Insights
- High overall loyalty.
- Monthly fluctuations indicate need for regular retention campaigns.
- Combined with RFM analysis, helps identify at-risk customers and increase repeat purchases.

---

## 📌 Strategic Takeaways

1. Business is stable but not growing → focus on AOV and retention.  
2. Revenue is evenly distributed across products and categories → low concentration risk.  
3. Top 20% of customers generate ~44% of revenue → personalized retention campaigns are key.  
4. Main revenue from card and online payments → Cash on Delivery carries higher risk.  
5. Discount impact is minimal → can optimize to improve margin.  
6. Geographic differences: TX and CA are leaders → focus marketing and operations there.  
7. High overall RPR (68.7%) → strong loyal customer base, monitor monthly fluctuations.
